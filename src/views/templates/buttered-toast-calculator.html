Toast Trajectory FAQ

  • Scale: Results run from 0% (miracle) to 100% (prepare napkins).
  • Spin boosters: Taller drops (H), a hearty shove (O), and extra butter (B) push the toast toward disaster.
  • Spin dampers: Dense bread (T), sticky air (F), and heavy gravity (g) slow the rotation.
  • Half-rotation sweet spot: Landing near 0.5 turns is the butter-down bullseye.

How It Works

This simplified rotation model estimates the toast's orientation after a fall by balancing height, push, butter weighting, drag, and inertia.

Tips for a butter-up landing

  • Change the drop height so the toast avoids the deadly half-turn rhythm.
  • Ease off the push; gentle slides reduce angular velocity.
  • Experiment with different breads or spreads to change T.
  • Consider not buttering both corners—balance matters.
